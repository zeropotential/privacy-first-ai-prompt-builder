How It Works

This free tool helps you write better instructions for AI chatbots like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini — no technical skills needed. Just fill in a few fields and we'll build an optimized prompt for you.

1

Describe What You Need

Use the form below to tell us what you want the AI to do, who it should sound like, and how you want the answer formatted. Don't worry — every field has examples to guide you.

2

Watch It Build Live

As you type, the "The Vault" panel on the right shows the finished prompt in real time. You'll also see how many tokens (word-pieces) it uses and what it would cost on different AI services.

3

Copy Your Prompt

Happy with the result? Click the Copy button. The prompt is now on your clipboard, ready to paste.

4

Paste Into Your AI Tool

Open your favourite AI chatbot and paste the prompt into the message box. The AI will follow your instructions precisely — see detailed steps below.

Where do I paste the prompt?

After clicking Copy, go to one of these AI chatbots and paste your prompt. Here's exactly how:

ChatGPT

  1. Go to chatgpt.com
  2. Click the message box at the bottom
  3. Paste your prompt (Ctrl+V or Cmd+V)
  4. Press Enter — that's it!

💡 For best results, paste into a new chat so previous messages don't interfere.

Claude

  1. Go to claude.ai
  2. Start a new conversation
  3. Paste your prompt into the message box
  4. Press Enter to send

💡 Claude is great with long, detailed prompts — don't be afraid to fill every field.

Gemini

  1. Go to gemini.google.com
  2. Click "New chat" in the sidebar
  3. Paste your prompt into the text box
  4. Press Enter to send

💡 Gemini is free with a Google account — a great place to start.

What is the CO-STAR-A framework?
C — Context: The background story. Tell the AI what situation it's helping with.
O — Objective: Your goal. What do you actually want the AI to produce?
S — Style: The expert persona. Should the AI sound like a lawyer? A marketer? A teacher?
T — Tone: The vibe. Formal for a report, casual for a blog post, persuasive for a pitch.
A — Audience: Who will read the output? The AI adjusts its language and depth to match.
R — Response: The shape of the answer — a list, a table, plain text, or even raw data (JSON).
A — Answer Constraints: Any extra rules — "keep it under 200 words", "include 3 examples", "don't mention competitors".
Tips for getting better results
  • Be specific — "Write a marketing email for a yoga studio launching a beginner class" is much better than "Write an email."
  • One goal per prompt — if you need three things, build three prompts. Trying to do everything at once confuses the AI.
  • Try Token Saver — flip the toggle in The Vault to automatically trim filler words. This saves you money on paid AI plans without changing your instructions.
  • Pick the right format — use Bullet Points for quick summaries, Table for comparisons, and Markdown for full articles.
  • Start fresh often — click the 🗑️ trash icon in the top-right corner to clear everything and begin a new prompt from scratch.
Your privacy is protected. Everything happens right here in your browser. We don't store your data, we don't use cookies, and we don't track you. When you close this page or click the trash icon, your work is gone forever — exactly as it should be.
